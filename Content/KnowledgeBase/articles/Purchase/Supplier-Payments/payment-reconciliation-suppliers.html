Payment Reconciliation for Suppliers

Payment reconciliation is the process of matching your payment records in EquiBillBook with bank statements, supplier statements, and other financial records to ensure accuracy and completeness. Regular reconciliation helps identify discrepancies, prevent errors, and maintain accurate financial records.

What is Payment Reconciliation?

Payment reconciliation involves comparing your recorded supplier payments in EquiBillBook with external records such as bank statements, supplier acknowledgments, and other financial documents to ensure all payments are accurately recorded and accounted for.

Why is Payment Reconciliation Important?

Payment reconciliation is crucial for:

  • Ensuring payment accuracy and preventing errors
  • Identifying missing or duplicate payments
  • Detecting fraud or unauthorized transactions
  • Maintaining accurate accounts payable balances
  • Meeting audit and compliance requirements
  • Improving cash flow management
  • Building trust with suppliers through accurate records

How to Reconcile Supplier Payments

Step 1: Gather Required Documents

Before starting reconciliation, collect:

  • Bank statements for the reconciliation period
  • Supplier statements or payment acknowledgments
  • Cheque registers (if applicable)
  • Payment vouchers and receipts
  • Payment reports from EquiBillBook

Step 2: Export Payment Reports

Generate payment reports from EquiBillBook:

  1. Navigate to Reports → Payables Reports
  2. Select "Supplier Payment Reports"
  3. Set the date range for the reconciliation period
  4. Export the report in Excel or PDF format

Step 3: Compare Records

Match payments systematically:

  • Compare each payment in EquiBillBook with bank statement entries
  • Match payment amounts, dates, and reference numbers
  • Verify supplier names and payment methods
  • Check for any missing payments in either record
  • Identify any discrepancies or unmatched items

Step 4: Identify Discrepancies

Common discrepancies to look for:

  • Missing Payments: Payments in bank statement but not in EquiBillBook
  • Unrecorded Payments: Payments made but not recorded in the system
  • Amount Differences: Payments with incorrect amounts
  • Date Differences: Payments recorded on wrong dates
  • Duplicate Payments: Same payment recorded multiple times
  • Wrong Supplier: Payments allocated to wrong supplier

Step 5: Resolve Discrepancies

Take action to resolve issues:

  • Record Missing Payments: Enter any payments found in bank statements but not in EquiBillBook
  • Correct Errors: Update incorrect payment amounts, dates, or allocations
  • Delete Duplicates: Remove duplicate payment entries
  • Reallocate Payments: Move payments to correct suppliers if misallocated
  • Investigate Issues: Look into unexplained discrepancies

Step 6: Mark Payments as Reconciled

After verification:

  • Mark matched payments as reconciled in the system
  • Add reconciliation notes for reference
  • Document any adjustments made
  • Save reconciliation reports for audit purposes

Reconciliation Frequency

Recommended reconciliation frequency:

  • Daily: For high-volume businesses or critical payments
  • Weekly: For most businesses to catch issues early
  • Monthly: Minimum frequency to match with bank statements
  • Quarterly: Comprehensive reconciliation for audit purposes

Using Payment Reports for Reconciliation

Key reports for reconciliation:

  • Supplier Payment Report: List of all payments made to suppliers
  • Supplier Statement: Complete transaction history for a supplier
  • Payment Method Report: Payments grouped by payment method
  • Date Range Report: Payments within a specific period
  • Outstanding Payables: Unpaid or partially paid bills

Bank Statement Import

If available in your system:

  • Import bank statements directly into EquiBillBook
  • Use automatic matching features to match payments
  • Review and confirm automatic matches
  • Manually match any unmatched items

Common Reconciliation Issues and Solutions

Issue 1: Payment Not Showing in Bank Statement

Possible Causes:

  • Payment date is in the future
  • Cheque not yet cleared
  • Payment not actually processed

Solution: Verify payment status, check clearing dates, confirm with bank

Issue 2: Payment Amount Mismatch

Possible Causes:

  • Data entry error
  • Bank charges deducted
  • Exchange rate differences (for foreign payments)

Solution: Correct the amount, account for bank charges, adjust for exchange rates

Issue 3: Payment Recorded to Wrong Supplier

Possible Causes:

  • Selection error during payment entry
  • Supplier name confusion

Solution: Reallocate payment to correct supplier

Best Practices for Payment Reconciliation

  • Reconcile regularly, preferably weekly or monthly
  • Use consistent date ranges for reconciliation periods
  • Keep all supporting documents organized
  • Document all adjustments and corrections
  • Review outstanding items from previous reconciliations
  • Use reference numbers to match payments easily
  • Reconcile by payment method for better organization
  • Maintain a reconciliation checklist
  • Have a second person review critical reconciliations
  • Keep reconciliation reports for audit purposes
