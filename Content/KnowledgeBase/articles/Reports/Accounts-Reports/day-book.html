Day Book

The Day Book is a chronological record of all financial transactions that occurred on a specific day in EquiBillBook. It provides a daily summary of all accounting entries, helping you track daily business activities and maintain accurate daily financial records. This guide explains how to generate and understand the Day Book.

What is a Day Book?

The Day Book shows:

  • All transactions for a specific date
  • Transaction types (Sales, Purchase, Payments, Receipts, Journal Entries, etc.)
  • Transaction details and references
  • Debit and credit amounts
  • Account-wise transaction summary
  • Daily totals for all transaction types

Purpose of Day Book

The Day Book is used to:

  • Review all transactions for a specific day
  • Track daily business activities
  • Verify daily transaction entries
  • Maintain daily accounting records
  • Identify missing or incorrect entries
  • Prepare daily financial summaries
  • Support daily cash reconciliation

Accessing the Day Book

To generate a Day Book:

  1. Navigate to Reports from the main menu
  2. Go to Accounts Reports
  3. Select Day Book
  4. Select the Date for which you want to view transactions
  5. Select branch (if using multi-branch setup)
  6. Configure additional filters if needed
  7. Click "Generate" or "View Report"

Understanding Day Book Structure

1. Transaction Types

The Day Book organizes transactions by type:

  • Sales Transactions: Sales invoices, sales returns, credit notes
  • Purchase Transactions: Purchase bills, purchase returns, debit notes
  • Payment Transactions: Customer payments, supplier payments, expenses
  • Receipt Transactions: Customer receipts, advances received
  • Journal Entries: Manual accounting entries
  • Bank Transactions: Bank deposits, withdrawals, transfers
  • Other Transactions: Adjustments, contra entries, etc.

2. Transaction Details

Each transaction shows:

  • Date: Transaction date
  • Reference Number: Invoice number, bill number, voucher number, etc.
  • Description: Transaction description or notes
  • Account: Account affected by the transaction
  • Debit Amount: Debit entry amount
  • Credit Amount: Credit entry amount
  • Transaction Type: Type of transaction

3. Daily Summary

The Day Book provides daily totals:

  • Total Sales: Sum of all sales transactions
  • Total Purchases: Sum of all purchase transactions
  • Total Payments: Sum of all payment transactions
  • Total Receipts: Sum of all receipt transactions
  • Total Debits: Sum of all debit amounts
  • Total Credits: Sum of all credit amounts
  • Net Balance: Difference between debits and credits

Day Book vs. Other Reports

Day Book vs. General Ledger

  • Day Book: Shows transactions for a specific day
  • General Ledger: Shows transactions for all accounts over a period
  • Day Book is date-specific
  • General Ledger is account-specific

Day Book vs. Account Book

  • Day Book: Shows all transactions for a day
  • Account Book: Shows transactions for specific account(s)
  • Day Book is chronological
  • Account Book is account-focused
