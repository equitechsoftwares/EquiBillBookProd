Aging Analysis (Payables)

Aging Analysis for Payables categorizes your outstanding supplier payments by how long they have been outstanding. This helps you identify overdue amounts, prioritize payments, and manage cash flow effectively.

What is Aging Analysis for Payables?

Aging Analysis provides:

  • Categorization of payables by age (0-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+ days)
  • Total amount in each aging bucket
  • Breakdown by supplier within each aging period
  • Overdue amount identification
  • Payment priority guidance
  • Trend analysis over time

Why Aging Analysis is Important

This analysis helps you:

  • Identify overdue payments quickly
  • Prioritize which payments to make first
  • Manage cash flow and payment schedules
  • Maintain good supplier relationships
  • Avoid late payment penalties
  • Plan payment budgets
  • Monitor payment performance
  • Identify payment trends

Aging Analysis Report Structure

The report typically displays:

  • Supplier Name: Each supplier with outstanding amounts
  • Current (0-30 days): Amount not yet overdue
  • 31-60 Days: Amount overdue by 31-60 days
  • 61-90 Days: Amount overdue by 61-90 days
  • Over 90 Days: Amount overdue by more than 90 days
  • Total Outstanding: Sum of all aging buckets per supplier
  • Grand Total: Total for each aging period across all suppliers

Understanding Aging Periods

Current (0-30 Days)

Amounts not yet overdue:

  • Within payment terms
  • Requires payment planning
  • Helps manage short-term cash flow
  • Should be paid on or before due date

31-60 Days Overdue

Amounts overdue by 31-60 days:

  • Requires immediate attention
  • May affect supplier relationships
  • Should be prioritized for payment
  • May require communication with supplier

61-90 Days Overdue

Amounts overdue by 61-90 days:

  • Critical payment status
  • High priority for payment
  • May impact future credit terms
  • Requires urgent payment planning

Over 90 Days Overdue

Amounts overdue by more than 90 days:

  • Highly overdue
  • Highest payment priority
  • May require special payment arrangements
  • Could significantly affect supplier relationships
  • May result in credit restrictions

Filtering Aging Analysis

You can filter reports by:

  • As-of Date: View aging as of a specific date
  • Supplier: Filter by specific suppliers
  • Location/Branch: Filter by business location
  • Supplier Category: Filter by supplier categories
  • Amount Range: Filter by outstanding amount range
  • Minimum Age: Show only amounts above certain age

Aging Analysis by Supplier

View aging breakdown for each supplier:

  • See which suppliers have overdue amounts
  • Identify suppliers with consistently late payments
  • Prioritize payments by supplier importance
  • Plan supplier payment schedules

Aging Analysis by Location

For multi-location businesses:

  • View aging by branch or location
  • Compare aging across locations
  • Identify location-specific payment issues
  • Allocate payment budgets by location

Key Metrics in Aging Analysis

  • Total Current: Total not yet overdue
  • Total Overdue: Sum of all overdue amounts
  • Overdue Percentage: Percentage of total that is overdue
  • Average Days Overdue: Average age of overdue amounts
  • Oldest Outstanding: Age of the oldest unpaid bill
  • Payment Coverage: Ability to cover overdue amounts
  • Supplier Concentration: Top suppliers by overdue amount
