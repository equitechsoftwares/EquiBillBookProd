Payment Reports (Payables)

Payment Reports for Payables provide comprehensive information about payments made to suppliers, helping you track payment history, analyze payment patterns, and manage supplier payment activities effectively.

What are Payment Reports for Payables?

Payment Reports provide:

  • History of all supplier payments
  • Payment amounts and dates
  • Payment methods used
  • Payment status and reconciliation
  • Payment trends and patterns
  • Outstanding vs. paid analysis

Why Payment Reports are Important

These reports help you:

  • Track payment history to suppliers
  • Monitor payment patterns and trends
  • Reconcile payments with supplier statements
  • Analyze payment methods and preferences
  • Verify payment accuracy
  • Plan future payments
  • Maintain payment records
  • Support audit and compliance

Accessing Payment Reports for Payables

  2. Select Payment Reports or Supplier Payment Reports
  3. Set the date range for payments
  4. Apply filters if needed (supplier, location, payment method)
  5. Generate the report

Payment Report Information

The report typically displays:

  • Payment Date: Date when payment was made
  • Supplier Name: Supplier who received payment
  • Payment Amount: Amount paid
  • Payment Method: Cash, bank transfer, cheque, online, etc.
  • Reference Number: Payment reference or transaction ID
  • Bills Paid: Purchase bills covered by the payment
  • Payment Status: Completed, pending, or failed
  • Reconciliation Status: Reconciled or unreconciled
  • Payment Notes: Additional payment information
  • Total Payments: Summary totals for the period

Filtering Payment Reports

You can filter reports by:

  • Date Range: Daily, weekly, monthly, or custom periods
  • Supplier: Filter by specific suppliers
  • Location/Branch: Filter by business location
  • Payment Method: Cash, bank, cheque, online, etc.
  • Payment Status: Completed, pending, or failed
  • Amount Range: Filter by payment amount range
  • Reconciliation Status: Reconciled or unreconciled

Payment Summary

The report provides summary information:

  • Total Payments: Grand total of all payments
  • Number of Payments: Count of payment transactions
  • Average Payment Amount: Average per payment
  • Payments by Method: Breakdown by payment method
  • Payments by Supplier: Total paid to each supplier
  • Payments by Location: Payments by branch/location
  • Payment Trends: Daily, weekly, or monthly trends

Payment Methods Analysis

Payment Reports vs. Other Reports

Payment reports complement other reports:

  • Payment Reports: History of payments made
  • Accounts Payable Summary: Current outstanding amounts
  • Aging Analysis: Age of outstanding amounts
  • Use payment reports for historical analysis
  • Use other reports for current status

Need Help?

If you have issues with Payment Reports:

  • Ensure payment entries are properly recorded
  • Verify payment dates and amounts are accurate
  • Check that payment methods are correctly selected
  • Ensure proper access permissions
  • Contact support if data seems incorrect
